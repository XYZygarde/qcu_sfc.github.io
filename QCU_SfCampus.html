About Us

We are the developers of this Informational Website about our Campus, QCU - San Francisco Branch. Our goal is to provide our fellow students, faculty, staff, and the wider community with a comprehensive and up-to-date resource about our institution. Our group of web developers, graphic designers, content writers, and project managers is focused on providing outstanding outcomes and are passionate about technology. We work closely together, exchanging ideas, brainstorming solutions, and putting in a lot of effort to improvise and refine the features and functionality of the website. The QCU San Francisco Campus website is a valuable resource for both prospective and current students, and we take pride in our work on it. Feel free to take some time to navigate through our website and discover more about our campus.

The developers are composed of 7 BSIT students, the project manager is Christian I. Ancog while the frontend developers are Jane Abalos, Ryza Sabordo, Christian Jan Gathchalian, Jojit Oliva Jr., Samuel Mendoza, and Jhon Lloyd Pasacsac.

Offered Programs

QCU San Francisco Campus offered two programs, Bachelor of Science in Information Technology (BSIT) and Bachelor of Science in Entrepreneurship (BS Entrepreneurship). They are both undergraduate degree programs that prepare students for careers in the technology and business sectors. BSIT program focused on the technical aspects of information technology, such as programming, networking, and cybersecurity. BS Entrepreneurship program focus on the business aspects of starting and running a company, such as marketing, finance, and management.

QCU LIBRARY

The Quezon City University - San Francisco library provides a vast collection of books, journals, and other resources to support the academic needs of the community. It offers various services to patrons, including reference services, circulation services, inter library loan services, computer and printing services, and instructional services. Librarians assist in finding information on various topics, while circulation services allow borrowing materials from other libraries. Additionally, the library provides computers for research and printing, and offers instruction on using library resources.

QCU CLINIC

The Quezon City University San Francisco Clinic offers basic medical and dental services to students and staff. Services include initial and comprehensive medical examinations, consultation and treatment for minor ailments, first aid and emergency care, dental check-ups, dental fillings and extractions, and health education and counseling. The clinic is staffed by licensed and experienced professionals committed to providing quality and compassionate care. To access services, students and staff can visit during its operating hours without appointments, but a valid QCU ID and medical history photocopy are required.

QCU COMPUTER LAB

The Quezon City University San Francisco Computer Laboratory (SFCL) is a modern facility that offers students access to the latest technology and software. It features various computer workstations connected to the QCU network and internet access. The lab also offers software applications like Microsoft Office and Adobe Creative Suite. The SFCL is staffed by IT professionals who can assist with troubleshooting, software installation, and printing. It is a valuable resource for QCU SF students, providing access to the latest technology, a quiet environment, IT assistance, and opportunities for collaboration. The SFCL is highly recommended for QCU SF students.

QCU FACULTY

QCU San Francisco's faculty is composed of highly qualified professionals dedicated to providing quality education to students. QCU FACULTY

The QCU SF Greenhouse is a modern facility that offers students the chance to learn about sustainable agriculture. It features a rainwater harvesting system, composting system, and hydroponic system. The greenhouse is used for growing fruits, vegetables, and herbs, conducting research on sustainable practices, and providing educational opportunities. It also serves as a source of fresh, locally grown produce for the QCU community. The QCU SF Greenhouse reduces the university's carbon footprint and promotes sustainable agriculture practices.
